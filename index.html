Planets

Jupiter

Jupiter is the largest planet in our solar system. It has a Great Red Spot, which is a giant storm that has lasted for hundreds of years.

Saturn

Saturn is famous for its beautiful ring system made of ice particles and dust. It's the sixth planet from the Sun.

Mars

Known as the Red Planet, Mars has fascinated humans for centuries. It's home to Olympus Mons, the largest volcano in our solar system.

Venus

Venus is the hottest planet in our solar system with a toxic atmosphere and temperatures that can melt lead.

Learn more

Neptune

Neptune is the eighth and farthest planet from the Sun. It has the strongest winds in the solar system, reaching speeds of 1,200 mph.

Learn more

Uranus

Uranus is the only planet that rotates on its side. It appears to roll around the Sun rather than spinning upright like the other planets.

Stars

Sirius

Sirius is the brightest star in the night sky. It's actually a binary star system located about 8.6 light-years away.

Learn more

Betelgeuse

Betelgeuse is a red supergiant star in the constellation Orion. It's expected to explode as a supernova someday.

Learn more

Vega

Vega is one of the brightest stars in the northern sky and is only 25 light-years away. It was once our North Star and will be again in about 12,000 years.

Learn more

Polaris

Polaris is our current North Star, located almost directly above Earth's north pole. It has helped navigators for centuries.

Learn more

Antares

Antares is a red supergiant star in the constellation Scorpius. Its name means "rival of Mars" due to its reddish appearance.

Learn more

Aldebaran

Aldebaran is an orange giant star located in the constellation Taurus. It appears to be the eye of the bull in the Taurus constellation.

Learn more

Constellations

Orion

Orion is one of the most recognizable constellations, featuring Orion's Belt, three bright stars in a row. It's visible from both hemispheres.

Learn more

Ursa Major

Ursa Major contains the Big Dipper, one of the most famous star patterns. It's useful for finding the North Star.

Learn more

Cassiopeia

Cassiopeia is a distinctive W-shaped constellation named after a queen in Greek mythology. It's located in the northern sky.

Learn more

Scorpius

Scorpius resembles a scorpion with its curved tail and is one of the zodiac constellations. It contains the bright red star Antares.

Learn more

Cygnus

Cygnus is known as the Swan constellation and features a cross-like shape often called the Northern Cross. It lies along the Milky Way.

Learn more

Pegasus

Pegasus features a distinctive square pattern known as the Great Square of Pegasus. It's named after the winged horse in Greek mythology.
